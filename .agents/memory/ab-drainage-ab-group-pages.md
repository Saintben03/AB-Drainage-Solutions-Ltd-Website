name: ab-drainage AB Group sub-brand pages (Construction/Fencing/Facilities)
description: How the 3 AB Group sub-brand pages differ from the main site's hero/section conventions — resolved after "too dark" feedback
---

The 3 sub-brand pages (`ABGroupConstruction.tsx`, `ABGroupFencing.tsx`, `ABGroupFacilities.tsx`) share an identical structure (hero → about → services → gallery → why-us → enquiry → footer banner), each with its own accent color (red `#dc2626`, green `#16a34a`, slate `#475569`/`#94a3b8`) and its own divider shape component (Diagonal/Organic/Stepped). This is a separate design system from the main ab-drainage site — do not confuse with `ab-drainage-hero-overlay.md` (which covers Home/Services/etc.).

**Resolved direction: bright hero + white/light body, NOT a flat dark veil.** Client explicitly rejected a full-frame `bg-[COLOR]/50` (mixBlendMode color) + `bg-black/45` (multiply) veil across the whole hero image as "mega dark throughout" — even though earlier feedback had asked to strengthen the filter. The fix that satisfied them: drop the flat full-image veil entirely; instead use directional gradients only (`bg-gradient-to-t from-black/75 via-black/10 to-transparent` + `bg-gradient-to-r from-black/60 via-black/10 to-transparent` for text legibility on the left, and a `bg-gradient-to-r from-[COLOR]/40 via-[COLOR]/5 to-transparent` color-blend layer concentrated only where the text sits). This keeps the image's own vivid color (yellow excavator, green garden, cool corridor) visible on the right/top of the frame — matching the "old site" reference where the hero image itself looks colorful and the dark-for-legibility treatment is a corner gradient, not a wash.

**Body sections alternate white and genuinely dark (near-black, e.g. `#0a0a0a`/`#0a0f0b`/`#0c0e10` per page) — NOT all white and NOT all dark.** First pass over-corrected to all-white/light-tint sections; client pushed back: "I didn't mean all the pages should be white, we need some dark colours in there and bright white." Resolved pattern per page: HERO(dark image)→ABOUT(white)→SERVICES(dark, `bg-zinc-900/border-zinc-800` cards, white text)→GALLERY(white)→WHY-US(dark)→ENQUIRY(white)→FOOTER BANNER(solid accent-color band). Divider `from`/`to` constants must be swapped in lockstep with each section flip (WHITE/DARK, not WHITE/LIGHT-tint).

**Why:** client said the pages were "too basic," "mega dark throughout" (v1), leading to an all-white/light-tint rewrite (v2) — then clarified v2 went too far the other way and they want real contrast: some sections bright white, some genuinely dark, not just a subtle light-grey tint standing in for "dark." The reference site alternates strong dark bands with crisp white ones.

**How to apply:** when a divider component's `from`/`to` params reference section colors (they take raw hex, not Tailwind classes), keep them in sync with whatever the adjacent section's actual background is — `WHITE = "#ffffff"` and a per-page near-black `DARK` constant, alternating between them section to section. Each page keeps its own divider SHAPE (Diagonal/Organic/Stepped) — only the from/to colors changed, not the divider identity.
